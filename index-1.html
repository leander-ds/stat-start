Two worlds. If nothing is going on (H₀) the test statistic lands in the left curve; if your effect is real (H₁) it lands in the right. The cutoff turns each into a decision. 

          

          

        

        

          
What am I looking at?

          
Every study is a bet made on one sample. The left curve is the world where the groups are truly identical (H₀); the right curve is the world where the effect is real (H₁). You pick a cutoff — past it you declare a finding. The red sliver beyond the cutoff under H₀ is a false alarm (α, Type I error); the amber area under H₁ that falls short of the cutoff is a miss (β, Type II error); the green area is power — your chance of catching a real effect. Move the sliders and watch the green grow.
